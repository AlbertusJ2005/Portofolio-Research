Machine Learning for Healthcare:
Diabetes Prediction

Evaluating machine learning algorithms and stacking ensemble approaches to improve early diabetes detection.

Overview

Diabetes is a chronic disease that continues to rise globally, requiring early detection to aid in prevention and medical decision-making. In this research, I collaborated on developing a machine learning model to predict diabetes risk using patient health data. The study focuses on comparing the performance of several algorithms and utilizing an ensemble learning approach to improve prediction accuracy. The findings were successfully published in the EMACS (Engineering, Mathematics and Computer Science) Journal.

Research Objective

The objective of this research is to evaluate the effectiveness of various machine learning algorithms in predicting diabetes and to identify the approach that yields the best performance. Furthermore, it aims to explore how ensemble learning methods can enhance prediction stability and accuracy compared to single models.

My Contribution

As a research team member, I was deeply involved in data processing, machine learning model development, experimental evaluation, data visualization, and drafting the scientific manuscript. This experience significantly strengthened my skills in data analytics, programming, academic research, and cross-disciplinary team collaboration.

Research Process

The research began with exploring the healthcare dataset to understand its characteristics and identify challenges, notably class imbalance. We then proceeded with data preprocessing, which included median value imputation, applying SMOTE to handle the class imbalance, and normalizing the data using Min-Max Scaling. Once the data was prepared, several machine learning models were trained and evaluated before implementing the Stacking Ensemble method.

Machine Learning Models

Three primary algorithms were utilized in this study: Logistic Regression, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), and Naive Bayes. These models were subsequently combined using a Stacking Ensemble approach to leverage the strengths of each individual algorithm, resulting in more stable and robust predictions.

Results & Impact

The results indicated that K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) delivered the best performance among the individual models, achieving an accuracy of 77.27%. However, the Stacking Ensemble approach, utilizing Logistic Regression as the meta-model, achieved the highest overall performance with an accuracy of 80.52% and an AUC score of 0.8604. This proves that combining multiple machine learning models can significantly elevate prediction quality.

The Impact: This research demonstrates that the application of machine learning and ensemble learning has immense potential to assist in the early screening of diabetes much faster and more efficiently. This approach can serve as a foundational layer for developing clinical decision support systems, particularly in environments with limited medical resources.
